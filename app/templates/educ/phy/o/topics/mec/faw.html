Force

Force is a push or a pull which changes the body's state of rest or uniform motion in a straight line. Force is vector Quantity which has both Magnitude and Direction. The SI Unit of Force is newton (N).

Definitions:

A newton

A newton is a force which gives a mass of 1kg an acceleration of 1ms-2. One newton is equivalent to one kilogram meter per square second.

Effects of Force

Force on a body has the following Effects;

  • It makes a stationary body move when pushed or pulled.
  • It changes the direction of a moving body.
  • It deforms or changes the shape of a body.
  • It changes the velocity of a moving body.
  • It stops a moving body.
  • It changes the speed of a moving body.

Types of Forces:

The following are the types of Forces;

  • Frictional Force

    This is the force that opposes relative motion between two surfaces.

  • Centripetal Forces

    This is the force that keeps a body moving in a circular path and this force is directed towards the center of the circle.

  • Up-Thrust force

    This is an upward force acting on a bod falling or immersed in a fluid. Fluids include; air, and any other liquid.

  • Magnetic force.

    This is the force that attracts or repels magnetic substances or materials.

  • Cohesion Force

    This is the force of attraction between molecules of the same substance/Kind.

  • Adhesion Force.

    This is the force of attraction between molecules of different substances.

  • Viscous Drag.

    This is the force which opposes motion of bodies in fluids.

  • Tensional Force.

    This is the pull or compression of the string at both ends.

  • Gravitational Force.

    This is the force that pulls a body towards the center of the earth. Gravitational force pulls all he bodies towards the center of the earth with the same acceleration called Acceleration Due To Gravity, g. Acceleration due to gravity is therefore the acceleration with which the gravitational force pulls the object on or near the earth's surface towards the center of the Earth. Hence; It is the rate of change of velocity for a body or object falling freely under gravity.

    The average value of g on the earth's surface is about 9.81ms-2 which is usually approximated to 10ms-2 for simplicity in calculations st The Ordinary Level of Education.

NOTE: Acceleration due gravity depends on the distance from the center of the Earth and since the Earth is not a perfect sphere, then, Acceleration due to gravity is Greater at the poles than at the Equator because the polar radius is less than the Equatorial radius.

RESULTANT FORCE:

The resultant force is a single force which has the same effect as of two or more forces acting on a body. When two or more forces act on a body there is a resultant force, the resultant force may be greater or equal to zero. When the resultant force on the body is zero the body is said to be equillibrium.

FRICTION

This is the force which opposes motion between two bodies in contact.

Types of Friction

There are two types of friction, namely;

  • Static friction.
  • Dynamic Friction.

Static Friction

This is the frictional force between objects or surfaces tending to slide against one another but they have not yet started moving. In-other-words, they are about to move/slide against each other. It occurs between stationary bodies or surfaces and depends on;

  • The co-efficient of static friction.
  • Normal reaction on the body.

Co-efficient of Static Friction, μ:

This is the ratio of limiting friction to the normal reaction. Limiting friction is the frictional force between two surfaces when they are at the verge of sliding over each other. When a body of mass, m rests on a rough surface, the following forces at on the body;

  • R - Normal reaction.
  • F - Frictional Force.
  • mg - weight of the body.
Where μ is called the Co-efficient of static friction
Note that;
  • The weight of the body acts downwards from the center of the Object.
  • The reaction(normal reaction) Acts from the bottom of the body, or from the Surface on which the body is resting.

Frictional Force, F on the surface depends on the following factors;

  • the nature of the surface,
  • the magnitude of the force moving the body,
  • the weight/mass of the body.

Experiment to determine the coefficient of friction:

  • Connect a light inextensible string to a block of wood resting on a rough horizontal surface.
  • Suspend a mass hanger on its other end by a light inextensible string which passes over a smooth fixed pulley at the edge of the surface.
  • Increase the force, F on the mass hanger until the block just begins to move.
  • Note and record the total force, F on the mass hanger.
  • Add standard weights at a time to the block of wood and repeat the above procedure to find the new total force F.
  • Tabulate your results in a suitable table.

  • A graph of F against R(R = W) is plotted and its slope determined which is the coefficient of friction, μ.

Example;

A block of mass 2kg is placed on a horizontal surface. Find the limiting friction if the coefficient of friction is 0.2.

Dynamic Friction

This is the frictional force between surfaces moving relative to each other. Dynamic friction depends on the following factors;

  • The weight of the moving object on top of the other surface.
  • The nature of the surfaces in contact
  • The co-efficient of Kinetic friction

Laws of Friction

  • Frictional force is directly proportional tot he normal reaction.
  • Frictional force is independent of the area of the surfaces in contact but depends on the nature of the surfaces.
  • Frictional Force is independent of the Relative velocity between the two surfaces in contact.
  • Frictional force always opposes relative motion.

Advantages of Friction

The following are the advantages of friction in the day to day life;

  • It enables movement of bodies.
  • It Enables us to walk.
  • It enables us to write.
  • It enables us to light matches from the match box.
  • It enables us to brush our teeth, shoes, floors, etc...
  • It prevents the foot of a ladder from slipping off.
  • It enhances the proper functioning of the braking system in cars, motor cycles, bicycles, and other moving vehicles on the ground.

Disadvantages of Friction

The following are the disadvantages of frictions. The things that make friction a nusance in our day to day life.

  • It cause unnecessary noise.
  • It causes surfaces to wear off.
  • It causes unnecessary heat between surfaces.
  • It retards movement of objects.
  • It cause energy wastage.
  • It reduces the efficiency of machines.

Ways of Reducing Friction

The following are the ways of reducing friction between any two surfaces;

  • Lubrication by using lubricants such as Oil and Grease.
  • Using ball bearings.
  • using rollers.
  • Smoothening the surfaces in contact.

WEIGHT

Definition:

This is the Force that the body exerts on anything that freely supports it. Or This is the gravitational pull acting on a body towards the Centre of the Earth. The Direction of weight is always towards the center of the Earth. Weight is therfore a vector Quantity and the SI Unit is newton (N)

Weight can also be define as the Product of the body's mass and the acceleration due to gravity for that body. Therefore;

Where g = acceleration due to gravity = 10ms-2

Differences between mass and Weight:

Mass Weight
i) Quantity of Matter in a Body The gravitational Pull on a Body
ii) Constant Every where Varies from place to place
iii) SI Unit is kilogram (kg) SI Unit is newton (N)
iv) It is a scalar Quantity It is a vector Quantity
v) It is a basic quantity It is a derived quantity
