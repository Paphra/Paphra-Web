Introduction


Physics

This is a branch of science that deals with the study of Matter and it's relation to Energy

Matter

This is anything that occupies space and has Weight. There are three states of Matter and these are; Solids, Liquids and Gases.

  • Solids are made of particles which are highly compacted and they are held together by strong forces of attraction.
  • Liquids are made of molecules loosely held together by Cohesion forces and Adhesion forces which makes then to flow to different regions bring about the fact that liquids find their own level.
  • Gases are made of molecules which are free to move apart from each other and occupy the space in which they are contained. Gas molecules attract each other by a very week force of attraction. Examples of gases may include; Oxygen [O2], Carbon dioxide [CO2], Nitrogen [N2], among others.

Time

This is the measure of duration between the occurrence of an event or a number of events. Time is usually measured in seconds and seconds is abbreviated as "s". Other units of measurement of time include; micro-seconds, Minutes, hours, days, weeks, forte-nites, months, years, decades and centuries, among others.

Energy

This is the ability to do Work. There are different forms of Energy and these are;
  1. Heat Energy
  2. Sound Energy
  3. Light Energy
  4. Chemical Energy
  5. Solar Energy
  6. Nuclear Energy
  7. Mechanical Energy

The Above forms of Energy are the main components or branches of the physics subjects and later on in on this site, we shall discuss each one of them individually. In this section, we are dealing with the mechanical part of the physics branch of science.

Scientific [Standard] Notation

This is a short way of expressing very big or very small numbers using the powers of 10. The number takes the form of a x 10n. Where a is a positive integer from (0 to 9) and n is an inter of any sign [positive or negative] and of any magnitude. Therefore, the decimal point is either moved from left to right for very small numbers less than 1 and the power of 10 [ in the above case (n)] is negative or from right to left for very big numbers greater than 9 and the power of 10 [n] is positive.

Note;
The power is Zero[0] if the given number is between 0 and 9 inclusive.

Examples;

1. Express the following numbers in scientific form;
  • 9
  • 450,000
  • 0.00056
Solution
  • 9
    • The number is greater than 1 but less than 10 thus n = 0 i.e the decimal point has moved no step.
    9 = 9.0x100
  • 450,000
    • The decimal point is after the last zero value thus it is moved to the left until it comes between 4 and 5 so that it is greater than 1 but less than 10. Count the number of times the decimal is moved and that is the value of n. i.e. n=5;
    450,000 = 4.5x105
  • 0.00056
    • The decimal point is moved from the left towards the right until it reaches between 5 and 6 so that it is greater than 1 but less than 10 The number of times moved by the decimal point towards the right is determined and that is the value of n.
    But since the Decimal point has moved from the left towards the right, the value of n becomes negative. Therefore;
    0.00056 = 5.6x10-4
NOTE:
  • When multiplying two or more numbers expressed in the standard form, add the powers of 10 and multiply the values of a. Do not multiply 10.
  • When dividing two or more numbers expressed in the standard form, subtract the powers of 10 and divide the values of a. Do not divide the values of 10

Examples:

  • 3x106 x 2x103 = 3x2x10(6+3)
    = 6x109
  • 3x106
