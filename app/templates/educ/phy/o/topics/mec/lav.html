LENGTH

This is the Distance between two points. It does not matter which path is taken. In-other words, irrespective of the Direction. The SI Unit of length is metre(m) but other units like millimeter(mm), centimeter(cm), kilometer(km), etc, can be used.

Converting Units of Length

Note that;
1km = 1000m
1m = 100cm
1m = 1000cm
1cm = 10mm

  • 200mm
  • 3km
  • 0.2cm

Measuring Length

Length can be estimated or accurately determined by using measuring instruments. There are various instruments for measuring length and th choice of the instrument is determined by the level of accuracy desired and the size of the object to be measured. These instruments include; Metre rule, tape Measure, Vernier Calipers and Micrometer Screw gauge.

Vernier Caliper

This is used to measure short distances in centimeters that is the length between 1cm and 10cm. It can be used to measure internal and external dimeters of the following;

  • Test Tubes
  • Small Pipes
  • Small round solids, and
  • Dimensions of other small solids and hollow Objects

The vernier caliper has two scales, the Main scale which is read in cm and the Vernier Scale which is read in mm divided into 10 equal divisions in length.

Measuring volume of Liquids

Liquids have no definite shape but assume the shapes of their containers in which they are put. The following instruments are used in measuring the volume of liquids;

  • Measuring Cylinder
  • Pipette
  • Burrete
  • Conical Flask
  • Beaker
  • Round Bottom Flask
  • etc...

Measuring cylinders are made of glass or transparent plastic and graduated in cm3 or ml and Pipettes, burretes, conical flasks and round bottom flasks are used to transfer known volumes of liquids.

Measuring the volume of an Irregular Object;

Volume of an Irregular Object/Solid is measured using the displacement method. The method works with solids that are not soluble in water i.e. that do not absorb water or do not dissolve in water.

1. Using a measuring Cylinder;

A measuring cylinder is an instrument, made of glass or plastic and it is calibrated or graduated with markings. Measuring cylinders come in different sizes, for-example, 200ml, 100ml, 400ml, 500ml, and so on. The water or any other liquid when poured into the measuring cylinder, the meniscus formed depends on the type of liquid which is poured into it. The meniscus formed by water is always a downward curved meniscus.

Procedure
  • Partly fill a measuring cylinder with water and take note of the initial Volume, V1, of the water.
  • Tie an irregular object [stone] with a thread and lower it gently into the cylinder until it is completely submerged and take note of the new Volume, V2, of the water.
  • The volume, V of the irregular object is calculated using the expression;
    • V = V2 - V2
2. Using a Eureka Can / Overflow Can;
Procedure:
  • Fill the Eureka can with water until it flows out of the spout and once the flowing stops, place an empty measuring cylinder under the spout of the can.
  • Tie the irreular object [stone], whose volume is to be determined, with a thread and lower it gently into the can until it is completely submerged.
  • The volume of the irregular object required is the volume of the water in the measuring cylinder.
