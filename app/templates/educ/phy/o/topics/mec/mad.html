Mass

Mass is the quantity of matter contained in a substance. The SI Unit of mass is kg. Other units include gram [g], milligrams [mg], tonnes, etc.


Measuring Mass

The Commonly used instruments for measuring mass include the following;

  • Top Balance
  • Beam Balance
  • Lever Balance
  • Weighing scale
  • Spring balance

Density

This is the Mass per unit volume of a substance. The SI unit of density is kilogram per cubic metre [kgm-3]. Another commonly used unit is gram per cubic centimeter [gcm-3]. The symbol used to represent density is ρ.

Factors affecting Density

The following are the factors that affect the density of a substance;

  • Temperature

    Density of a substance is inversely proportional to the temperature, that is the hight the Temperature, the lower the Density of the substance and the lower the Temperature, the higher the Density of the substance. This is because, when the temperature increases, the molecules or atoms of the substance expand leading to the increase in the volume of that substance and the lower the higher the volume of the substance, the lower the density since the mass remains constant. When the temperature decreases, the substances molecules or atoms contract hence decreasing the volume of that particular substance, and since the mass remains constant, the Density of that substance increases.

  • Pressure

    Pressure only affects the density of gases. For a fixed mass of gas, density is directly proportional to the pressure. This means that, at a higher pressure, the higher the density of the gas. This is because when the pressure of a mass of a gas is increased, it's volume reduces and since the mass remains constant, the density decreases and when the pressure reduces, the volume of the gase increases and since the mass remains constant, it's density decreases.

  • Concentration of Atoms

    In some materials, atoms are closely packed like in solids, thus a material made of atoms of a lower atomic is heavier than a material made of higher atomic number. This is because, atoms are larger and more spread apart hence a smaller density.

  • Change of State

    When a substance changes state, it's volume changes and since the mass is constant, then the Density of the substance changes.

Note:

Size and shape of the substance do not affect the Density because the arrangement of atoms or molecules inside the object or substance remains the same even if the size and shape are changed since they do not affect the packing of molecules or atoms in the substance.

Uses of Density

The following are the uses of Density;

Density Of Mixtures

A mixture is obtained by putting together tow or more substances that do not react with one another. The density of the mixture depends on the masses of it's constituent substances and their proportions. The volume of the mixture is the sum of the volumes of the individual constituents and mass of the mixture is the sum of the masses of the substances.

Relative Density.

Definition:

This is the ratio of the Density of a substance to the density of water.

Relative Density has no Units because it is a ratio of the Similar Quantities.
