Work Power And Energy - Exercise

WORK

Definition:

This is the product of force and distance moved in the direction of force. OR Work is done when the point of application of force moves in the direction of force. The SI Unit of work is a joule (J). A Joule is the work done when a force of 1N moves through a distance of 1m in the direction of force.

If the body is projected vertically, the force acting is the weight of the body.

Examples;

1. An object is pulled through a distance of 2m by a force of 55N. Calculate the work done.

2. An object is pulled through a distance of 20cm by a force of 1500N. Calculate the work done.

3. An Object of mass 500g moves through a vertical height of 40cm. Calculate the work done.

4. A body of mass 250g climbs 25 steps each of height 20cm. Calculate the work done.

POWER

Definition;

This is the rate of doing work.

The SI Unit of power is a watt (W). A watt is the rate of working of one joule per second. Hence 1W = 1Js-1 Other unnits of power include kilowatt (kW) and megawatt (MW).
1kW = 1,000W
1MW = 1,000,000W

Examples;

1. An engine raises 20kg of water through a height of 50m in 5 seconds. Calculate the power of the engine.

2. What is the power of the crane which can lift a box of mass 1000kg vertically through a height of 15m in 40 seconds?

3. A boy whose mass is 60kg can run up a flight of 28 steps ech of 25cm high in 56 seconds. Calculate the power developed by the boy.

ENERGY

Definition:

This isthe ability to do work. The SI Unit of energy is joule (J).

Forms of Energy:

  • Heat Energy

    This is the form of energy that is transfered from a region of high temperature to a region of low temperature. This form of energy is produced by burning fuels, electric heaters and radiation from the sun.

  • Sound Energy

    This is a form of energy produced when particles in the medium are set into vibrations. This form of energy is heard by the ear. It travels in forms of waves.

  • Electrical Energy

    This is a form of energy developed due to the flow of charges. This form of energy can be obtained by the conversion of other forms of energy using generators.

  • Light Energy

    This is the form of energy produced by hot bodies and travels in a straight line. This form of energy can be converted into other forms of energy and helps us to see.

  • Chemical Energy

    This is a form of energy that can be converted to heat by burning.

  • Nuclear Energy

    This is a form of energy produced when unstable nucleus splits through nuclear fission or two light nuclei fuse together through nuclear fusion.

  • Wave Energy

    This is a form of energy which is transfered from one point to another without causing any permanent displacement of medium itself. This form of energy causes a disturbance through the medium.

Sources of Energy:

  • The Sun.

    The sun produces light energy and heat energy which are used in different ways.

  • Wind.

    Wind is moving air which can drive turbines to produce electrical energy.

  • Fuel.

    Fuels like kerosene and petroleum when burnt produces heat energy and light energy which can be used in different ways.

  • Geothermal.

    Geothermal is a source of electrical energy in power stations.

  • Waterfalls or Dams.

    Waterfalls may be used to turn turbines in hydro-electric power stations.

  • Nuclear.

    When unstable nucleus splits, nuclear energy is released.

  • Oceans.

    Ocean currents can be converted into other forms of energy like electrical energy.

Renewable (Non-Exhaustible) sources of energy:

These are sources of energy which can be re-used to produce energy. Examples are wind, sun(solar), water (hydro-electric).

Non-Renewable (Exhaustible) sources of energy:

These are sources of energy which cannot be re-used to produce energy. Examples are kerosene, petroleum, coal, and biogas.

MECHANICAL ENERGY

This is divided into two forms of energy, namely;

  • Kinetic Energy.
  • Potential Energy.
Kinetic Energy.

This is the energy possessed by a body due to it's motion. It depends on the speed of the body.

Kinetic Energy = ½ x mass x (velocity)2
= ½mv2
Examples;

1. A body of mass 3kg moves with a speed of 30ms-1. Find it's kinetic energy.

K.E = ½mv2
= ½ x 3 x 302
= ½ x 3 x 900
= ½ x 2700
= 1350J

2. An Object of volume 100cm3 and density 8gcm-3 moves with a speed of 10ms-1. Find its kinetic energy.

K.E = ½mv2
m = ρ x v
= 8 x 100
= 800g
K.E = ½ x 0.8 x 102
= ½ x 0.8 x 100
= ½ x 80
= 40J

Potential Energy

This is the energy possessed by a body by virtue of it's position in the gravitational field.

Potential Energy = mass x Acceleration due to gravity x height
= mgh
Examples;

1. Find the potential energy possessed by a body of mass 350g when it is 10m above the ground.

P.E = mgh
= 0.35 x 10 x 10
= 35J

2. A 5kg mass falls from a height of 20m. Calculate the potential energy lost.

P.E = mgh
= 5 x 10 x 20
= 100J

The Law/principle of Conservation of Energy

It states that energy can neither be created nor destroyed but changes from one form to another.

Energy Changes:

1. For a body falling freely, the following energy changes take place;
P.E P.E + K.E
K.E Sound + heat.

2. For a freely swinging pendulum, the energy changes are;
P.E P.E + K.E
K.E P.E + K.E P.E

3. When lighting a match box;
Chemical energy Heat + Light.

4. When a boy compresses the spring;
Mechanical energy Elastic Potential Energy.

5. When lighting a lamp connected to a battery;
Chemical energy Electrical energy Heat + Light.

6. Catapult Pulled by a person to propel a stone;
Mechanical energy Electrical Potential energy Kinetic Energy.

Note:

For a body falling freely, its kinetic energy before impact is equal to potential energy above the ground.
